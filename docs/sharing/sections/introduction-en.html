Introduction back to ToC

The ontology suite for the Urban IoT Devices was developed to support the integration and the publication of different data from different service providers who manage the IoT devices in the urban area. The ontologies were developed for the Comune di Milano (Italy), but they are generic and can be applied to any city. The Sharing Mobility module ([http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/sharing](http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/sharing)) defines classes and properties related to the Sharing Mobility service providers, to the vehicles for sharing and to the sharing station as urban IoT devices, to the service users, and to the data collected from the devices on their usage. The concepts defined from the Sharing Mobility module reuse and extend the Core module ([http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/core](http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/core)) which is imported from the ontology. The modeling of the Sharing Mobility module was implemented with the contributions of domain experts from the Comune di Milano, and with the consultation of relevant standards for the domain, in particular the Mobility Data Specification ([MDS](https://github.com/openmobilityfoundation/mobility-data-specification)) and the General Bikeshare Feed Specification ([GBFS](https://github.com/NABSA/gbfs)). The Sharing Mobility module was implemented by reusing extensively the vocabulary [schema.org](http://schema.org), extending it to support specific requirements on the domain of interest. The module reuses the ontology Sensor, Observation, Sample, and Actuator ([SOSA](https://www.w3.org/TR/vocab-ssn/)) for the modeling of the IoT devices and the data collected from them. Furthermore, the [Core Vocabularies](https://ec.europa.eu/isa2/solutions/core-vocabularies_en) defined from Semantic Interoperability Centre Europe (SEMIC.EU), the [Time Ontology](http://www.w3.org/2006/time), the [Dublin Core](https://www.dublincore.org/specifications/dublin-core/dcmi-terms/) and the [GeoSPARQL](https://www.ogc.org/standards/geosparql) are reused. The Sharing Mobility module is complemented with the definition of SKOS vocabularies used in the ontology: - Car Supply [vh-kos:car-supply](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/vehicle/car-supply) - European Emission Standard [vh-kos:european-emission-standard](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/vehicle/european-emission-standard) - Propulsion Kind [vh-kos:propulsion-kind](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/vehicle/propulsion-kind) - Vehicle Category [vh-kos:vehicle-category](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/vehicle/vehicle-category) - Sharing Mobility Vehicle State [sh-kos:vehicle-state](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/sharing/vehicle-state) - Sharing Mobility Vehicle Event [sh-kos:vehicle-event](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/sharing/vehicle-event) - Dock State in Sharing Station [sh-kos:dock-state](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/sharing/dock-state)
