Introduction back to ToC

The ontology suite for the Urban IoT Devices was developed to support the integration and the publication of different data from different service providers who manage the IoT devices in the urban area. The ontologies were developed for the Comune di Milano (Italy), but they are generic and can be applied to any city. The Core module ([http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/core](http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/core)) defines classes and properties related to the service providers, to the IoT devices, to the service users, and to the data collected from the devices on their usage. The concepts defined from the Core module are expressed at high level and are declined in the specific domain inside the other modules in the suite that imports it (for example, Sharing Mobility module ([http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/sharing](http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/sharing)) and Electric Mobility module ([http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/electric](http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/electric)). The Core module was implemented by reusing extensively the vocabulary [schema.org](https://schema.org), extending it to support specific requirements on the domain of interest. The module reuses the ontology Sensor, Observation, Sample, and Actuator ([SOSA](https://www.w3.org/TR/vocab-ssn/)) for the modeling of the IoT devices and the data collected from them. Furthermore, the [Core Vocabularies](https://ec.europa.eu/isa2/solutions/core-vocabularies_en) defined from Semantic Interoperability Centre Europe (SEMIC.EU), the [Time Ontology](http://www.w3.org/2006/time) and the [Dublin Core](https://www.dublincore.org/specifications/dublin-core/dcmi-terms/) are reused.
