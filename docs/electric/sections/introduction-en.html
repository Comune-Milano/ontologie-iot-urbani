Introduction back to ToC

The ontology suite for the Urban IoT Devices was developed to support the integration and the publication of different data from different service providers who manage the IoT devices in the urban area. The ontologies were developed for the Comune di Milano (Italy), but they are generic and can be applied to any city. The Electric Mobility module ([http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/electric](http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/electric)) defines classes and properties related to the Electric Mobility service providers, to the charging infrastructure and to the charging station as urban IoT devices, to the service users, and to the data collected from the devices on their usage. The concepts defined from the Electric Mobility module reuse and extend the Core module ([http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/core](http://www.w3id.org/urban-iot/core)) which is imported from the ontology. The modeling of the Electric Mobility module was implemented with the contributions of domain experts from the Comune di Milano, and with the consultation of relevant standards for the domain, in particular the dell'Open Charge Point Interface protocol [OCPI](https://github.com/ocpi), and API available from different operators. Furthermore, the decree published on charging services was also consulted [DECRETO LEGISLATIVO 16 dicembre 2016, n. 257](https://www.gazzettaufficiale.it/eli/id/2017/01/13/17G00005/sg). The Electric Mobility module was implemented by reusing extensively the vocabulary [schema.org](https://schema.org), extending it to support specific requirements on the domain of interest. The module reuses the ontology Sensor, Observation, Sample, and Actuator ([SOSA](https://www.w3.org/TR/vocab-ssn/)) for the modeling of the IoT devices and the data collected from them. Furthermore, the [Core Vocabularies](https://ec.europa.eu/isa2/solutions/core-vocabularies_en) defined from Semantic Interoperability Centre Europe (SEMIC.EU), the [Time Ontology](http://www.w3.org/2006/time), the [Dublin Core](https://www.dublincore.org/specifications/dublin-core/dcmi-terms/), the [GeoSPARQL](https://www.ogc.org/standards/geosparql), and e for the plugin types modeling the [Mobivoc](http://schema.mobivoc.org/#) are reused. The Electric Mobility module is complemented by the definition of SKOS vocabularies used in the ontology: - Charge Access Method [el-kos:charge-access-method](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/electric/charge-access-method) - Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (EVSE) Charge Category [el-kos:EVSE-charge-category](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/electric/EVSE-charge-category) - Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (EVSE) State [el-kos:EVSE-state](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/electric/EVSE-state) - Parking Restriction [el-kos:parking-restriction](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/electric/parking-restriction) - Power Supply [el-kos:power-supply](http://w3id.org/urban-iot/kos/electric/power-supply)
